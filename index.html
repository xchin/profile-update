What sets me apart?

Three words. Customer driven innovation.
Being a designer and developer allows me to assess a user's needs and challenges.
As I create and evolve a design, it gives me an appreciation for intended flows and interactions.
While developing, I prefer to follow BEM methods and lint my javascript to ensure clean, legible code.
Accessibility is a must as well, as so I like to employ ARIA for compliancy.

PayPal: Hackathon Concept - Selfie Checkout

User Experience Design

Hypothetical user flow for PayPal's "selfie checkout". A concept for cart checkout processes that uses a selfie for PayPal account association.

Pages:  1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8 9

This flow starts on merchant sites with the PayPal purchasing buttons.

If "selfie checkout" is enabled on the user's account, the user is prompted for camera access to take the photo.

Once camera is enabled, framing is overlaid on photo making it easier to get an accurate photo.
Progress meter surrounding camera view allows users to focus on keeping face in frame.

Once an accurate photo is captured it is compared against a user's other profile pictures captured at time "selfie checkout" was enabled.

PayPal purchase, shipping and funding instrument review screen.

Option to edit shipping details or funding instrument in sliding sidebar.

Returns user to merchant for purchase confirmation and receipt.

Sad path: Error on photo comparison mismatch.

Sad path: Traditional sign-in option available in error flow.
